## Background

A multi-specialty medical group with 50 providers across 4 New Jersey offices reached out to GITSOLS after a partner-level conversation about IT risk. Their internal compliance lead had flagged three open issues: aging on-prem infrastructure, weak access controls, and a Business Associate Agreement (BAA) gap with a vendor that had recently been acquired.

The group&apos;s incumbent IT partner was reactive and good at break-fix. Strategic work was not happening.

## Discovery

Our 2-week discovery sprint covered four areas:

- **Infrastructure** — server age, virtualization platform, switching, Wi-Fi, EHR latency.
- **Identity & access** — Microsoft 365 tenant config, MFA coverage, conditional access, privileged account isolation.
- **Backup & recovery** — what was backed up, retention, last documented restore test.
- **Compliance posture** — HIPAA Security Rule control mapping, BAA inventory, training records.

The discovery report had 47 findings. 9 were P0.

## Engagement scope

We proposed a 90-day engagement with 7 milestones:

| Milestone | Duration | Owner |
|---|---|---|
| 1. Stabilize MFA and conditional access | 2 weeks | PM + Engineer |
| 2. Replace primary file server with M365 SharePoint + Teams | 3 weeks | PM + Engineer |
| 3. Migrate EHR database to a hosted Azure instance | 3 weeks | Engineer + EHR vendor |
| 4. Deploy EDR across all endpoints | 1 week | Engineer |
| 5. Update BAAs across vendor list | 2 weeks | PM + Compliance |
| 6. Rebuild backup posture with documented restore drill | 2 weeks | Engineer |
| 7. Internal audit dry-run | 1 week | PM + Compliance |

Each milestone had explicit deliverables and a client-approval gate.

## Execution highlights

**MFA in 14 days.** Coverage went from 22% to 100% by enforcing conditional access in tranches — physicians first (with phone-call MFA as a fallback for the older staff who pushed back), then operations, then admin. Every escalation had a documented exception and an expiry date.

**Zero-downtime EHR move.** The EHR migration ran Saturday → Sunday with two hours of read-only mode and a planned 4-hour write outage. We hit the window with 30 minutes to spare. Monday saw normal appointment volume and 38% lower latency on the heaviest forms.

**BAA inventory.** The vendor list expanded from 11 known vendors to 23 actual vendors that touched ePHI. Eight had stale BAAs; three had none. All resolved before milestone 5 closed.

**Restore drill that mattered.** Instead of "we ran a test," we restored the previous week&apos;s EHR database into a sandbox and had the compliance lead verify three actual patient records. That artifact became the centerpiece of the internal audit dry-run.

## Outcomes

- **0 appointments lost** to downtime during the 90-day engagement.
- **EHR latency reduced 38%** on the most-used clinical workflow.
- **MFA coverage 100%** within 14 days of milestone 1 kickoff.
- **Internal audit cleared** in time for cyber-insurance renewal — premium held flat at renewal despite hardening market.
- **Time to onboard new provider** reduced from 4 business days to half a business day.

## What we&apos;d do differently

The vendor inventory took longer than scoped. In the future we would start the vendor discovery in parallel with milestone 1 rather than scheduling it as milestone 5 — by the time we got there, contracts had already lapsed at one of the smaller vendors and we had to fast-track replacements.

## What&apos;s next

The group is now on our managed retainer. Monthly reports show ticket SLA, milestone progress on the post-engagement roadmap, and a continuous compliance dashboard. Next major project — a custom patient-intake portal — is in discovery as a [bespoke software](/services/bespoke-software) engagement.
